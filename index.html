Seung Won Baik

is a journalist and creative based around New York City. Video Editor for the New School Free Press. You can find me on Soundcloud, Twitter and Instagram.

You can also learn more about me here.

Lost in Transit

I produced a video featuring two fellow student commuters at the New School. With Don Eim and reporting by Ryanne Salzano.

An Industry Awakening: Out With Fast Fashion, In With Sustainability

This is a longer collaborative project, looking into the recent trend of farm-to-table fashion and slow fashion in response to the harms of the fast fashion cycle. I contributed two videos and photos, and assisted with reporting and editing the story.

Longtime Staff Honored in First University-Wide Ceremony

In practicing quick turnaround reporting, I contributed an article on a recognition ceremony for staff who have given more than 10 years of service to the university. Photo by Don Eim.

Loving Pains: A Podcast on Love

On Valentine's Day 2017, I launched the first podcast series on the New School Free Press. This podcast explores love and all of its mysteries from a student's point of view. With Don eim. Illustration by Alex Gilbeaux.

South Korean International Students Caught Between Conflicts

With Trump ascending to the U.S. presidency and South Korea's Park being impeached, South Korean international students at the New School express their concerns before soon returning to their country for winter break. I reported the story and co-produced the video. With Don Eim.

A Graffiti Urban Dictionary

I interviewed graffiti artists to personally explain the terms and slang that they use amongst crews. This is one part of a longer feature on graffiti and street art in New York City. With Mischka Capriles and Yasmin Leutwyler.

1 William Street, New York City

Assigned a random block in New York, I visually reported the area, creating graphics and taking photos of the buildings around 1 William Street.

Students Celebrate Love In Washington Square Park In Response to Election

Students from NYU, The New School and other universities around New York City gathered in the rain at Washington Square Park after the 2016 Presidential election. I reported the scene, taking pictures and interviewing participants or onlookers, for the New School Free Press.

Bringing Some Old School To The New School

I contributed a piece on alumni who return to work at the New School for a special issue by the New School Free Press. I interviewed four alumni, who each provided their perspective on their time at the university as a student. Illustration by Alex Gilbeaux. With photos by Julia Himmel.

Performing Arts Students No Longer Play To Empty Audiences

After hearing a student's complaint that not many attend concerts at Mannes, I reported a story on audience turnout for orchestral concerts at the university. To my surprise, I found out that actually the turnout has increased and that the orchestra actually do gather a quite sizeable audience for their concerts. Photo by Julia Himmel.

Seniors Are Ready to GTFO - Graduate the Fuck Out

I edited and contributed a short audio narrative featuring various seniors to Julia Himmel's photo story on graduating seniors at the New School.

New School Budget Cuts Are Limiting Student Work Opportunities

Hearing that students are losing jobs at the university, I reported out my first story for the New School Free Press. Photo by Julia Himmel.

Let's Talk About This Election, Shall We?

This was a class assignment that set a challenge to speak with strangers on the streets, to find contacts through Twitter, and to follow up with a topic. I spent some hours at Washington Square Park before and after the presidential election and documented people's voices. I also speak with Clinton Canvassers from out-of-state, including Florida and Michigan.


Coffee-Me

In this personal project, I explore my addiction to coffee. I interviewed friends and strangers from different parts of my life to answer this one question. Along with the audio piece, I produced a video to illustrate the narrative.

Passing Through The Bugaboos

I try to present a story visually by annotating a photo taken by my father, an avid rock-climber, who had recently returned from his two-week trip to British Columbia to climb Snowpatch Spire.

Get Lost In Katz's Delicatessen

I explored 'nostalgia' as it exists at Katz's Delicatessen, an iconic restaurant located in New York City's lower east side. I contributed two articles that discuss the history of Katz's and the strange surge of youth that flood Katz's tables in contrast to its old interior. With Sydney Oberfeld and Molly Mintz. Featured Photo by Sydney Oberfeld.

"Encouraging Passion" - letters from New York

I produced a three-part audio piece that features strangers from around New York City to try and encourage listeners to pursue their passions. Each part was created to represent an emotional stage in one's life journey.

Nineteen

In my first attempt at a long-form audio narrative, I produced a piece featuring two women, Sophie and Marge, who both share their unique perspectives as someone who is nineteen and someone who once was. With Allie Griffin and Jake Seferian.

Where is home for you? Why?

Displaced in an unfamiliar city, I pondered on this question and decided to create my first audio narrative work. Edited on ProTools.
