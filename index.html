An Industry Awakening: Out With Fast Fashion, In With Sustainability

This is a longer collaborative project, looking into the recent trend of farm-to-table fashion and slow fashion in response to the harms of the fast fashion cycle. It included a visit to a farm where they produced materials from llamas, alpacas, and other animals.

Loving Pains: A Podcast on Love

With Don Eim, I launched the first podcast for the New School Free Press. This podcast explores love and all of its mysteries from a student's point of view. Illustration by Alex Gilbeaux.

South Korean International Students Caught Between Conflicts

With Trump ascending to the U.S. presidency and South Korea's Park being impeached, South Korean international students at the New School express their concerns before soon returning to their country for winter break. With Don Eim.

A Graffiti Urban Dictionary

I interviewed graffiti artists to personally explain the terms and slang that they use amongst crews. This is one part of a longer feature on graffiti and street art in New York City. With Mischka Capriles and Yasmin Leutwyler.

1 William Street, New York City

Assigned a random block in New York, I visually reported the area, creating graphics and taking photos of the buildings around 1 William Street.

Students Celebrate Love In Washington Square Park In Response to Election

Students from NYU, The New School and other universities around New York City gathered in the rain at Washington Square Park after the 2016 Presidential election. I reported the scene, taking pictures and interviewing participants or onlookers, for the New School Free Press.

Bringing Some Old School To The New School

I contributed a piece on alumni who work at the New School to a special issue for the New School Free Press. I interviewed four alumni, who each provided an in-depth retrospective to their time at the university as a student. Illustration by Alex Gilbeaux. With photos by Julia Himmel.

Performing Arts Students No Longer Play To Empty Audiences

After hearing a student's complaint that not many attend concerts at Mannes, I reported a story on audience turnout for orchestral concerts at the university. To my surprise, I found out that actually the turnout as increased and that the orchestra actually do gather a quite sizeable audience for their concerts. Photo by Julia Himmel.

Let's Talk About This Election, Shall We?

This was a class assignment that set a challenge to speak with strangers on the streets, to find contacts through Twitter, and to follow up with a topic. I spent some hours at Washington Square Park before and after the presidential election and documented people's voices. I also speak with Clinton Canvassers from out-of-state, including Florida and Michigan.


Coffee-Me

In this personal project, I explored my addiction to coffee. I interviewed friends and strangers from different parts of my life to answer one question. Then, I set out to visually capture their stories.

Passing Through The Bugaboos

In practicing my visual reporting, I try my hand at annotating a photo. I interviewed my father, an avid rock-climber, who had recently returned from his two-week trip to British Columbia to climb Snowpatch Spire.

Get Lost In Katz's Delicatessen

With Sydney Oberfeld and Molly Mintz, I explored 'nostalgia' as it exists at Katz's Delicatessen, an iconic restaurant located in New York City's lower east side. I contributed two articles that discuss the history of Katz's and the strange surge of youth that flood Katz's tables in contrast to its old interior. Photo by Sydney Oberfeld.

"Encouraging Passion" - letters from New York

I created a three-part audio piece that features strangers from around New York City to try and encourage listeners to pursue their passions. Each part was created to represent an emotional stage in one's life journey.
